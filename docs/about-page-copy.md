## Why it exists
We are in the midst of an exciting time in the nuclear industry. There is a constant stream of news, announcements, and commentary coming at us from all directions. The challenge is separating signal from noise.
Important stories are often buried beneath repetition, partial summaries, and headline volume. By the time distinct developments begin to come into focus, much of the effort has already gone into filtering rather than thinking. The deeper insight is often there, but just out of reach.
Finding Critical Path exists to reduce that friction.
It is built around a simple idea: if the work of gathering, sorting, and narrowing the field can be done more effectively, more attention can be spent on analysis and understanding. What happened is crucial, but what it may signal, what connects across the week, and where the industry may be moving next are the insights we need to see what is ahead.
Each edition is designed to look broadly across developments in the nuclear industry, pull forward the stories shaping the conversation, summarize what matters, surface patterns across the week, and point readers back to the original sources for deeper exploration.
The goal is not to replace the news. It is to make it easier to make sense of it.
Finding Critical Path is an independent publication written by Josh Bell.

## Methodology
Finding Critical Path is built to help separate signal from noise across a fast-moving stream of nuclear industry news.
Each edition begins with a broad scan of relevant news stories and articles from across the nuclear landscape. From there, the process narrows. Repeated or overlapping stories are filtered down, distinct developments are surfaced, and a smaller set of meaningful items is selected for closer review.
The goal is not to capture everything equally. It is to identify the stories that appear to matter most, look more carefully at what they may indicate, and consider how they connect to the broader direction of the industry.
From that review, each edition is assembled around a small number of underlying signals. These may point to shifts in policy, supply chain development, financing, deployment, regulation, technology, industrial capacity, or other patterns shaping what comes next.
The scan draws from a mix of nuclear industry reporting, regulatory updates, policy developments, utility and energy coverage, and adjacent technology and industrial reporting. The aim is not to aggregate headlines, but to review credible reporting broadly enough to spot the developments and patterns that deserve closer interpretation.
The publication is designed to remain connected to original reporting. Each edition includes a Dive Deeper section that points readers back to the source material for additional context and fuller exploration.
The process uses digital tools to help gather, organize, and sort information more effectively. Those tools support a clearer, more useful read on the week’s developments and what they may mean.
